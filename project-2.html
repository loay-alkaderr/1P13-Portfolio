Project 2: Q-Arm w/ Surgical Container.

Project Overview:

Project "Get A Grip" focuses on two primary objectives to enhance project efficiency and ensure the safe transportation of a container. The modeling team is entrusted with the initial main objective, which involves developing a lightweight and medical-grade 3D-modeled container. This requires careful consideration of factors such as weight optimization for efficient transportation and adherence to medical-grade standards to ensure cleanliness and sterility.

Simultaneously, The computing team is tasked with the second main objective, creating a robust computer code for the Q-arm. This code is essential for executing multiple commands involved in transferring the container. The application plays a pivotal role in ensuring the precise execution of orders, facilitating a seamless transfer to the autoclave for sterilization without disruptions, and maintaining strict adherence to safety requirements throughout the entire process.

Together, These teams contribute to the overall success of Project "Get A Grip" by combining design excellence with advanced computing capabilities to achieve optimal project outcomes.

Project contribution:

Time Line: Oct, 26th - Dec, 6th 2023

As the leader of this initiative, I played a vital role in tackling the unique challenges faced by a less developed village with restricted access. Utilizing my expertise, I focused on enhancing the efficiency of the turbine blade through Computer-Aided Design (CAD). Employing my skills in AutoCAD, I meticulously designed a turbine that prioritized simplicity, durability, and the incorporation of locally-sourced materials.


One of my key responsibilities was to choose the optimal material for the turbine blade, taking into consideration factors such as availability, cost-effectiveness, and durability. This necessitated a thorough analysis of locally-sourced materials to ensure not only straightforward production but also easy maintenance, aligning with our commitment to sustainability.

Project Reflection:

Throughout the second project, Effective task and responsibility management grew in significance in me. I considered multiple questions, " In what ways can I assist in the submission of CAD files and major group files?" This perspective inspired a more streamlined methodology that emphasized timely contributions and the Modelling sub-team's joint efforts.

In addition, I actively participated in the secure container's design process, always questioning, "How can we meet the specifications for weight, minimal space, and ultimate safety of the project?" These self-posed concerns influenced our decision-making, ensuring that the container could safely keep the medical tool.
