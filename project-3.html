Project 3: Revenge of the Recycling System

Project Overview:

The project aimed to develop a comprehensive sorting and recycling system, merging virtual and physical realms to enhance waste management efficiency and promote sustainability. In the virtual domain, the computing team programmed the Q-bot's actions, employing line-following and color sensors to guide it to designated bins. In the real world, the same principles were applied, ensuring a smooth transition from virtual simulation to practical implementation.

The modeling team designed a mechanism utilizing a linear actuator to lift and retract sorting bins, facilitating precise waste sorting. Overall, our project seamlessly integrated virtual and physical components to create an automated sorting and recycling solution.

Project contribution:

Time Line: January, 2nd - Feb, 26th 2024.

As project lead, I played a pivotal role in orchestrating team efforts and ensuring cohesive collaboration between the computing and modeling sub-teams. My leadership and guidance fostered an environment of innovation and problem-solving, driving the project towards its goals effectively. My strategic decision-making and ability to navigate challenges were instrumental in overcoming obstacles and achieving milestones throughout the development process. My dedication and expertise were evident in every aspect of the project, from conceptualization to execution, making a significant contribution to its success.


One of my key responsibilities was to choose the optimal material for the recyling waste, taking into consideration factors such as availability, cost-effectiveness, and durability. This necessitated a thorough analysis of locally-sourced materials to ensure not only straightforward production but also easy maintenance, aligning with our commitment to sustainability.

Project Reflection:

Throughout this project, positive results were dependent on efficient timetable management and, most importantly, excellent communication. I thought to myself, "How can I organize the project's timeline to achieve optimal efficiency?" This investigation led to a better understanding of time management tactics. Organized group meetings emerged as a critical approach for increasing productivity and ensuring deadline compliance.
